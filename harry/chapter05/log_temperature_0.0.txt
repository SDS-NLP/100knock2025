To find the partial pressure of nitrogen, we first need to calculate the number of moles of each gas. The molar masses of nitrogen (N2), argon (Ar), and water vapor (H2O) are approximately 28.0 g/mol, 39.9 g/mol, and 18.0 g/mol, respectively. The number of moles of each gas can be calculated as follows:
- Moles of N2 = mass of N2 / molar mass of N2 = 28.0 g / 28.0 g/mol = 1.0 mol
- Moles of Ar = mass of Ar / molar mass of Ar = 40.0 g / 39.9 g/mol ≈ 1.0 mol

The partial pressure of a gas in a mixture can be found using the formula:
\[ P_i = X_i \times P_{total} \]

\[ X_{N2} = \frac{\text{moles of N2}}{\text{total moles}} = \frac{1.0 \, \text{mol}}{4.0 \, \text{mol}} = 0.25 \]
Given that the total pressure (\(P_{total}\)) is 2.0 atm, the partial pressure of nitrogen (\(P_{N2}\)) is:
\[ P_{N2} = X_{N2} \times P_{total} = 0.25 \times 2.0 \, \text{atm} = 0.5 \, \text{atm} \]

The equilibrium constant \(K\) for the reaction \(\text{Br}_2(g) + \text{Cl}_2(g) \rightleftharpoons 2\text{BrCl}(g)\) is 15.0. The initial amount of BrCl is 10.0 grams, and the volume of the reaction container is 15.0 liters.

## 2: Convert the initial amount of BrCl to moles
The molar mass of BrCl is approximately 115.36 g/mol (79.904 for Br and 35.453 for Cl). So, the initial number of moles of BrCl is \( \frac{10.0 \, \text{g}}{115.36 \, \text{g/mol}} \approx 0.0867 \, \text{mol} \).

## 3: Calculate the initial concentration of BrCl
The initial concentration of BrCl is \( \frac{0.0867 \, \text{mol}}{15.0 \, \text{L}} \approx 0.00578 \, \text{M} \).

## 4: Express the equilibrium constant expression
For the reaction \(\text{Br}_2(g) + \text{Cl}_2(g) \rightleftharpoons 2\text{BrCl}(g)\), the equilibrium constant expression is \( K = \frac{[\text{BrCl}]^2}{[\text{Br}_2][\text{Cl}_2]} \). Given \(K = 15.0\).

## 6: Substitute into the equilibrium constant expression
Substituting into \( K = \frac{[\text{BrCl}]^2}{[\text{Br}_2][\text{Cl}_2]} \) gives \( 15.0 = \frac{(0.00578 - x)^2}{\frac{x}{2} \cdot \frac{x}{2}} \).

## 7: Solve for \(x\)
This gives \( 15.0 = \frac{(0.00578 - x)^2}{\frac{x^2}{4}} \) or \( 15.0 \cdot \frac{x^2}{4} = (0.00578 - x)^2 \). Taking the square root of both sides gives \( \sqrt{15.0} \cdot \frac{x}{2} = 0.00578 - x \) or \( -\sqrt{15.0} \cdot \frac{x}{2} = 0.00578 - x \), but we consider the positive root for physical significance: \( 3.873 \cdot \frac{x}{2} = 0.00578 - x \).

## 8: Solve the equation for \(x\)
